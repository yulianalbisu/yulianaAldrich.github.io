QC and QA Testing

Making sure the product quality by continuous integration

Quality Control and Quality Assurance take center stage in the Agile development process, ensuring products are tested in small, continuous batches through continuous integration. This approach not only saves time and money but also allows for the timely detection and resolution of errors in subsequent deployments.

I love Agile, because it follows my values making me deliver quality of work, keeping me on track with the technologies, leading the team following the same goal, support each other, mentor when is needed and measure goals. But if you need more to choose Agile here it goes:

Benefits of QA in Agile

Avoiding Requirements Misunderstandings: - Flexibility in Agile allows for quick adjustments, serving as a communication bridge between stakeholders and developers.
Usability Testing and Customer Feedback: Early user engagement facilitates usability testing and clarifies feature requests, enhancing the overall value stream.
Continuous Integration for Bug Discovery: Continuous integration evaluates code fragments for potential bugs or defects, fostering ongoing improvements.
Agile Team Productivity Reporting: Regular sprint reporting provides insights into productivity and suggests areas for enhancement, fostering communication between the tech team and stakeholders.


Scrum and Kanban Approaches: Embracing Scrum and Kanban methodologies ensures transparent sprint planning, effective backlog management, and progress visualization.
Test Techniques and Continuous Integration Practices: Leveraging INVEST technique prioritizes defects and ensures a non-negotiable focus on crucial tasks.
Benefits of Continuous Integration: Early detection, regular feedback, reduced regression risk, and increased visibility into progress contribute to successful feature development.
Automation for Efficiency: Automation, exemplified by tools like Cypress, addresses repetitive tasks, saving time and resources.
Release Planning Expertise: Quality Assurance Engineers contribute to release planning by defining testable user stories, estimating testing efforts, and participating in quality risk analyses.
Test Automation and System Aspects: Identifying functional and non-functional aspects, participating in multiple levels of testing, and supporting test automation enhance the overall quality of the development process. improvements.

So in conclusion ...

In essence, a Quality Assurance Engineer in the Agile landscape is a pivotal figure, ensuring not just the functional integrity of a product but also contributing to the collaborative and iterative nature of the development lifecycle.
